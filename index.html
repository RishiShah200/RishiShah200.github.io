Robotics Building

Initially, we decided to have a 4 wheel drive with 2 motors. The back 2 wheels has motors which was geared to the front two wheels. Because of this design, we were not able to clib platform as there was not enough torque and the robot moved slowly.

We later realized that in order for us to be able to climb the center platform, we must change the drive. I decided to make it a 6 wheel drive instead of 4 with 4 motors. This is to allow for easy manuverability with the robot while driving. I chose to motor the back 4 omni wheels and chain the front wheels with a 12-tooth sprocket on both sides. Additionally, the tank drives allows for more room to add additional components to the robot and was easy for me to program. Ultimately, making a 4 motor tank drive allowed for more power which in turn helped our robot climb the center platform better. This is the same design that we used for the Banquet Night Bot.

Robotics Building

I built a fork claw and cap rotator so that we can rotate the cap while it is in our possession. Instead of using the normal 2 standoffs, i built it suing 4 so that we could effectively flip the cap instead of trying to balance it on two standoffs. I connected the cap rotator to a motor using two 24 tooth sprockets which were chained to each other in norder to flip the cap in the desired direction. An additional benifit of this is we were able to pick up caps that were tilted on the field. While we did not end up keeping this on the robot after the first competiton, it was effective while we used it but ultimately used our harvester to flip caps instead.

Robotics Building

After we decided to remove the cap flipper/rotator that I built, we decided to use our harvester intake to flip caps as well. I made the intake roller by using two 30 tooth sprockets which were connected to each other by 4 standoffs in the center of the rollers. The rollers were connected using nylocks, ensureing that they would not come apart when coming in contact with caps or balls and providing structural integrity. As a team, we decided to cover the rollers in mesh to make sure entanglement did not occur with the other bots on the fiels which can lead to a disqualification.

Banquet Night Robot

For the banquet night robot, we decided to build a flywheel to shoot the balls as it is more efficient and shoots faster. We geared the flywheel from one side of the robot for speed to allow the ball to shoot the necessary distance required to hit the flags. I built the flywheel and adjusted the intake to allow for the balls to enter the shooter.

At this point, the flywheel is done. except for the platform the ball needs to wrap around.

Video of flywheel running.

Due to time constraints, I was unable to attach the platform the ball needed for the ball to wrap around and shoot. Instead, I decided to attach a simple linear puncher to the robot as we only have 1-2 days before the event.
